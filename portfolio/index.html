Seeing the Unseen: SeeNN Multimodal ML System for Atmospheric Visibility Estimation #

  • Rowan University & Federal Aviation Administration USA

Overview:

Our ongoing project focuses on developing an advanced multimodal machine learning system tailored for in-flight long-range atmospheric visibility estimation. The system leverages RGB (color) and Depth data, collected from various sources like cameras and depth sensors, to accurately predict visibility conditions during flight.

Our multimodal machine learning system, designed and trained using TensorFlow and Keras, serves as a valuable tool for in-flight long-range atmospheric visibility estimation. By combining RGB and Depth data in a unified model, we aim to provide accurate and reliable visibility predictions during flight operations. The application of this technology extends to various domains, including aviation, environmental monitoring, and weather prediction, ensuring safer and more efficient air travel experiences.


System Overview:

Our first step involves data preprocessing, where we clean, handle missing values, and align the RGB and Depth data to ensure smooth fusion during the training phase. TensorFlow and Keras are our primary tools for building the machine learning model.

Model Architecture:

The core of our system is a sophisticated multimodal deep learning architecture that seamlessly integrates the RGB and Depth information. The RGB model consists of Convolutional Layers, Pooling Layers, and a Flatten layer, while the Depth model mirrors the RGB model's structure, ending in a cross-attention multimodal fusion technique. These models are trained using TensorFlow and Keras, enabling us to optimize their performance efficiently.

Mermaid Diagram: Model Architecture

Application Flow

Once trained, our system is ready for real-world applications. During in-flight operations, pilots and relevant personnel can interact with the system through a user-friendly interface. By providing images or video streams containing RGB and Depth data, they initiate the visibility estimation process. The input data undergoes preprocessing and is then fed into the trained multimodal model for accurate visibility prediction.


DeepArganiaWatch: A Patch-Based Deep Learning System for Change Detection of Argania Deforestation using Multispectral Sentinel-2 #

  • @IRF-SIC

Overview:

DeepArganiaWatch is an advanced project that focuses on developing a Patch-Based Deep Learning System for change detection of Argania deforestation using Sentinel-2 multispectral imagery. The project comprises two major components: DeepArganiaDetector, a deep learning segmentation model specifically designed for Argania tree detection, and a change detection module that utilizes DeepArganiaDetector to detect and visualize deforestation changes between two dates specified by the user.

DeepArganiaDetector:

The foundation of DeepArganiaWatch lies in the development of DeepArganiaDetector, a deep learning segmentation model. DeepArganiaDetector is trained to take 64x64 pixel patches from Sentinel-2 multispectral imagery, consisting of 13 channels. It performs semantic segmentation to isolate Argania trees by outputting a 64x64 binary mask where "1" denotes the presence of Argania trees and "0" represents the background.

Training DeepArganiaDetector:

DeepArganiaDetector is pre-trained on the EuroSAT dataset using a U-Net architecture, producing an output of 64x64x13 for Argania tree, The last layer of the model is then modified to output a 64x64x1 binary mask to perform accurate Argania tree segmentation on our custom dataset. The model is fine-tuned on our dataset to achieve precise segmentation of Argania trees.

Change Detection Module:

The Change Detection module is an essential part of DeepArganiaWatch. Users input two dates and a 64x64 region of interest. DeepArganiaDetector is applied separately to the Sentinel-2 imagery from each date, producing two binary masks representing Argania tree presence for each date. The change map is then generated by analyzing the differences between the two binary masks. This process allows us to visualize and quantify deforestation changes in the specified region over time.

DeepArganiaWatch - Advancing ArganiaWatch:

DeepArganiaWatch is an improved version of ArganiaWatch, enhancing the accuracy and performance of change detection through the utilization of the DeepArganiaDetector model. By employing deep learning techniques and multispectral Sentinel-2 imagery, DeepArganiaWatch provides a more precise and comprehensive analysis of Argania deforestation, empowering researchers and environmentalists to make informed decisions for conservation and sustainable land management.

Conclusion:

DeepArganiaWatch represents a significant advancement in change detection of Argania deforestation using multispectral Sentinel-2 imagery. The integration of DeepArganiaDetector, a powerful deep learning segmentation model, allows us to precisely identify Argania trees and visualize changes in the tree cover over time. This project's impact extends to environmental conservation efforts, providing valuable insights to policymakers and researchers in preserving and safeguarding the Argania tree population. DeepArganiaWatch builds upon the foundation of ArganiaWatch, enabling more accurate detection of deforestation changes and enhancing our understanding of the dynamic landscape of the Argania trees in the Souss region.

ArganiaWatch: A Machine Learning System for Change Detection of Argania Deforestation using Sentinel-2 #

  • @IRF-SIC

Overview:

ArganWatch is an innovative project that focuses on developing a cutting-edge Machine Learning System for change detection of Argania deforestation using Sentinel-2 satellite imagery. Leveraging the powerful ArganiaDetector model, previously developed for Argania tree detection, we feed it geolocation data and extract Sentinel-2 time series values from 2015 to 2022. By analyzing the temporal changes in the pixel values, the system accurately determines whether the Argania trees have been affected by deforestation or are still intact.

System Overview:

The foundation of the ArganWatch project is the ArganiaDetector model, which has been trained on Sentinel-2 pixel values to identify Argania trees with a high accuracy rate of 90%. To repurpose this model for change detection, we feed it specific geolocation data, and it analyzes Sentinel-2 time series values covering the period from 2015 to 2022. The system then detects changes in the Argania tree cover, helping identify potential deforestation areas.

Machine Learning Approach:

The ArganWatch system utilizes the TensorFlow Decision Forest library, which powered the original ArganiaDetector model, to perform change detection. The model processes the Sentinel-2 pixel values extracted from the time series data and predicts whether Argania trees have been deforested or are still present. By capitalizing on the pre-trained model, we efficiently repurpose it for a new application, saving computational resources and time.

Application Flow:

The ArganWatch application follows a structured flow to detect changes in Argania tree cover:

  1. User Input: Users provide geolocation data through the application interface.

  2. Time Series Data Extraction: The system retrieves Sentinel-2 imagery time series data for the specified location from 2015 to 2022.

  3. Feature Extraction: The ArganiaDetector model processes the time series data, extracting relevant features for change detection.

  4. Change Detection: Using the trained model, the system analyzes the extracted features and predicts whether Argania trees have been deforested or are still existing.

  5. Results and Reporting: The application provides users with detailed reports and visualizations, highlighting the detected changes and potential deforestation areas.

Conclusion:

The ArganWatch project introduces a powerful Machine Learning System for change detection of Argania deforestation using Sentinel-2 satellite imagery. By repurposing the ArganiaDetector model, we efficiently analyze temporal changes in pixel values to detect potential deforestation areas from 2015 to 2022. The system's accurate change detection capabilities offer valuable insights to researchers, environmentalists, and policymakers, facilitating timely interventions for preserving and conserving the Argania tree population. ArganWatch emphasizes the importance of monitoring and protecting Argania trees, contributing to sustainable land management and promoting a greener and healthier environment for future generations.

RGB-Based ML System for Atmospheric Visibility Estimation #

  • Rowan University & Federal Aviation Administration USA

Visibility degradation is the cause of many road accidents and air crashes around the globe, it is caused and controlled by multiple key factors that hinder the observer from having a clear vision of what's ahead, playing a crucial role in aviation safety. Alas, researchers and engineers created many tools to measure or restore images suffering from those degradations. This paper compares different image-based deep learning architectures for Atmospheric Visibility Range Classification. A Vision Transformer trained from scratch and three pre-trained Convolution Neural Network (CNN) models were examined and contrasted in terms of accuracy, obtaining a validation accuracy of more than 95 %. Our experiment shows that the different models trained on the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) initial dataset can be used towards building an efficient tool to aid in Flight Control for long-range visibility estimation. Results showed that DenseNet121 was the best-performing model with 99 % training accuracy and 98 % validation accuracy and showed early convergence, while the Vision Transformer kept on showing signs of improving but falling behind only by a bit behind DenseNet121 by the end of the experiment.

Argania Forest Change Detection From Sentinel-2 Satellite Images using U-Net Architectures #

  • @IRF-SIC

Land Cover Change Detection (LCCD) is an important task from the perspective of regional resource monitoring, land use and environmental planning. One of the major concerns for maintaining biodiversity is deforestation. Meanwhile, the recent advances in the field of image processing by Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and the wide availability of high spatiotemporal resolution satellite imagery provide an unprecedented configuration for the implementation of effective and scalable forest cover change monitoring systems. The objective of this study is to evaluate the performance of two of the most effective deep learning architectures, namely, U-Net and Improved U-Net for the mapping of forest cover to identify deforestation areas between two dates. To this end, two 10m resolution satellite images from the Sentinel-2 satellite covering the Argania forest, a UNESCO Biosphere reserve in the Souss-Massa region in Morocco were used. The implemented methods involve training CNN with two different patch sizes (32–16) in a post-classification framework to compare the classification maps and deduce the change map. The classification accuracy of the two models reached 100% with an advantage to U-Net with an average of Intersection-Over-Union (mIoU) equal to 0.851. The obtained results indicated that Argania forest areas were decreasing over the years, while residential areas and greenhouses were expanding.
