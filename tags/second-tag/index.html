Jake of all trades, Master of Data Science.

I have always been fascinated by the beauty and power of computers, and I have always wanted to understand it, be part of it, and innovate. To pursue this interest, I taught myself the basics of computer science using online courses and books. Since English is not my first language, the road was a bit rough at first, but I persevered and eventually obtained my high school degree in mathematics. As of today, I became a Research Fellow in Rowan University.

My Philosophy
in a Nutshell

From a young age, I found myself faced with the daunting task of making decisions without guidance. I relied on my intuition, dedication, and determination to overcome the obstacles in my path. I will not deny that I have faced many failures, but I have learned from them and ensured that I do not repeat the same mistakes. In the process of navigating these challenges, I have developed a passion for knowledge and a love of learning that has shaped my current character and contributed to my humble achievements.

I am grateful for the hardships that have shaped me, for they have helped me to cultivate a deep appreciation for the beauty and complexity of the world.

Impressive, right?
or more like...Imposter?

I believe that the feeling of being an imposter is a natural part of the learning process. Many of us have experienced this sensation, whether it was in elementary school when we were first introduced to new concepts and felt overwhelmed, or in middle school when we discovered that we were capable of mastering previously daunting tasks. In those moments, we may have felt doubt, confusion, and fear, but these feelings are simply a normal part of growth and development.

Rather than letting the fear of being an imposter hold us back, we should embrace it as an opportunity to continue learning and improving. Those who are truly committed to lifelong learning will never stop striving to better themselves, and they will not let the fear of being an imposter stand in their way. Instead, they will use it as motivation to keep pushing forward, knowing that with each new challenge they overcome, they are one step closer to achieving their goals.

w/ @kaitodev, 4th July 2022, Agadir, Morocco
